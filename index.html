vested
product cofounder

a finance app that helps you save money by not thinking about saving money

▹ about
In late 2018, a friend and I set out to tackle what we saw as a rising problem—people have a hard time saving money. We believed that by creating what we like to call a FitBit for saving, we could encourage healthy savings behaviors. We began by hosting a series of interviews to gain insights and validate our assumptions. After that, I began a serties of rapid prototyping iterations. This cycle repeated several times until we felt like our MVP was ready for development. I handed off an interactive prototype and design assets to a dev agency. On a daily basis I am in contact with our dev team to ensure quality and iron out issues. As of this writing, the app is in early alpha as we continue to test and prepare for launch.

silo
personal project

a productivity tool designed specifically for the apple watch to help you focus and get things done

▹ about
This began as a project for a consumer wrist watch with built-in haptic technology. Around the same time, the Apple Watch was released so I pivoted and started building an app instead. The real breakthrough was the design of the tick markers. Something about the red on black countdown ticks gave you a feeling that you were getting stuff done. The simplicity of the app seemed to resonate with a target audience. Silo became a quick a success on the App Store. I continue to maintain and update the app each year.

helm
personal project

what if you could wake up inside your dreams and explore a side of you that is reserved for the sleep world

▹ about
A longtime goal of mine has been to create an app for exploring dreams. I had a gut feeling that there was something powerful about our dreams. If you have ever had a lucid dream, you realize that it is quite a unique experience. While there are well documented techniques of inducing lucid dreams, it is cumbersome to say the least. In this project I plan to make it easier to not only attain lucidity, but to turn a dream into a virtual experience that you can learn from.

TEDx
collaboration

a collaborative design effort for a logo and asset rebranding of Boston’s TEDx Youth organization

▹ about
In this assignment, I volunteered with a small team of four designers to redesign a logo and branding material for TEDx Youth. We were given 12 hours to complete the task. It started with each of us sketching out our own ideas and then sharing one time with the client. After some raw feedback, we began designing the best ideas we had. The result was indeed a collaboritive one and showcased the power of team work. One of the designers had unknowingly sketched what looked to be the shape of the X, meanwhile another designer noticed that the X could be both an X and Y all in one, meanwhile a third designer had the vision to make a beautiful execution of the logo.
